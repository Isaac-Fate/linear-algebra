Preface#

Linear algebra is a fascinating field of mathematics that offers a powerful set of tools for modeling and analyzing real-world problems. From designing computer graphics to solving systems of equations, linear algebra has numerous applications in science, engineering, and technology. With its combination of abstract theory and practical applications, linear algebra offers a unique opportunity to explore the interplay between mathematics and the real world.

The main structure of this book follows Sheldon Axler’s Linear Algebra Done Right[Axler, 1997], which emphasizes understanding vector spaces and linear transformations before introducing matrices and determinants. However, I believe that more examples and applications related to matrices can help students better understand the subject, and therefore I aim to supplement Axler’s approach with additional content in this area.

This book covers a wide range of topics, starting from basic vector space theory and linear systems to more advanced topics such as eigenvalues, singular values, and applications in data science and machine learning. By providing both theoretical and practical insights, I hope to offer readers a comprehensive and integrated understanding of the subject.
